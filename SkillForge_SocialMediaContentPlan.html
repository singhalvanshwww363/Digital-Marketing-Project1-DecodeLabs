Content Pillars

Three core pillars drive all content. Marketing is not noise — it is Decision Relief.

1

Problem-First Thought Leadership

LinkedIn B2B — stop selling the solution. Start validating the problem. Explain WHY a pain point exists, not just how to fix it.

2

UGC & Social Proof

Instagram B2C — real photos outperform professional shots. Showcase authentic intern visuals building AI Dashboards and real apps.

3

The Art of the CTA

Both platforms — use "Start", "Get", "Claim" not "Submit". Breadcrumb technique + social proof next to every CTA.

Pillar 1 — LinkedIn Details

Stop selling the solution. Start validating the problem. Content must explain WHY a problem exists (e.g., "Why LMS fatigue is hurting adoption") rather than just announcing a course.
  • Post types: Data-backed infographics, text posts, carousel slides
  • Why it works: Education buyers don't want features; they want to know you understand their constraints (budget, compliance, political complexity).
  • CTA style: "What's the #1 skill gap in your campus?" — conversation starter, not a sales pitch.

Pillar 2 — Instagram Details

Real photos outperform professional shots. Showcase 'Imperfect' visuals — interns working on Deep-Stack projects like AI Dashboards.
  • Post types: Reels, carousels, behind-the-scenes stories
  • Psychological trigger: Peer Validation. Seeing peers deploy real apps is the strongest social proof.
  • CTA style: "Tag a friend who needs this" / "DM us to join the next batch"

Pillar 3 — CTA Rules

Avoid "Submit." Use low-friction verbs like "Start," "Get," or "Claim." Always place social proof next to the button to reduce anxiety.
  • LinkedIn CTA: "Start My Career Track — Verified by AICTE"
  • Instagram CTA: "Claim Your Seat — Next Batch Starts July 7"
  • Breadcrumb: Ask for small commitments (choose your domain) before the big ask (enroll).
